New Vocabulary: Technical Terms

Machine Learning

Machine Learning is when computers learn from examples instead of being explicitly programmed with rules.

üéØ Think of it like this: Instead of telling a computer "if the image has pointy ears and whiskers, it's a cat," you show it 1,000 pictures of cats and 1,000 pictures of dogs, and it figures out the difference on its own.

Example: TikTok's algorithm learns what videos YOU like by watching what you watch, like, and skip. It doesn't have rules like "show everyone dance videos" - it learns YOUR preferences!

New Vocabulary: Technical Terms

Training Data

Training Data is the collection of examples you use to teach a machine learning model what to recognize.

üìö Think of it like this: Training data is like flashcards. If you want to learn Spanish, you need lots of example words and phrases. If you want AI to recognize your face, you need lots of pictures of your face from different angles.

Important: The more examples (and more varied) your training data is, the smarter your AI becomes. Bad training data = bad AI!

What is Machine Learning? ü§ñ

Machine Learning (ML) is how we teach computers to recognize patterns and make decisions based on data, rather than following pre-programmed rules.

üéµ Real-World Example: Spotify

Spotify's AI analyzes tracks for tempo, energy, danceability, and acousticness. That's how it knows you might like a new song even if you've never heard it before - it learned what musical patterns you enjoy!

You Use Machine Learning Every Day:

  • TikTok's For You Page - Learns what you like within minutes
  • Snapchat Filters - Recognizes your face and features
  • YouTube Recommendations - Predicts what you'll watch next
  • Photo App Face Sorting - Groups pictures by person automatically
  • Voice Assistants - Understand your voice and accent

How Does Machine Learning Work?

The 3-Step Process:

  1. üéì Training Phase: You show the AI lots of examples
    • Show 100 pictures of cats labeled "cat"
    • Show 100 pictures of dogs labeled "dog"
    • The AI finds patterns: pointy ears, whiskers, size, etc.
  2. üß™ Testing Phase: You show it NEW examples it hasn't seen
    • Show a new cat picture (without the label)
    • AI makes a guess: "That's a cat!"
    • You tell it if it's right or wrong
  3. üöÄ Prediction Phase: The AI is ready to use in the real world
    • Now it can identify cats vs dogs it's never seen before
    • The more it practiced, the more accurate it is

Real Example: How TikTok's AI Works

üéØ Mind-Blowing Stat:

The Result: Within minutes of using TikTok, the algorithm identifies your niche interests and serves content you'll probably love - even from creators you've never heard of!

Introducing: Teachable Machine! üéâ

What is Teachable Machine?

A FREE tool by Google that lets you train your own AI models in your web browser - no coding required!

Why It's Awesome:

  • ‚úÖ 100% Free - No cost, no subscription
  • ‚úÖ No Login Required - Start immediately
  • ‚úÖ Works in Browser - Nothing to download
  • ‚úÖ Privacy-Friendly - All training happens on YOUR computer
  • ‚úÖ No Coding Needed - Just point, click, and train
  • ‚úÖ Export Your Model - Use it in websites or apps

Three Types of Projects:

  1. üì∏ Image Project - Teach it to recognize objects, gestures, faces
  2. üéµ Audio Project - Teach it to recognize sounds, voices, music
  3. üßç Pose Project - Teach it to recognize body positions and movements

How to Use Teachable Machine

Step-by-Step Demo Guide:

  1. Go to the website: teachablemachine.withgoogle.com
  2. Click "Get Started"
  3. Choose a project type: Image, Audio, or Pose
  4. Create classes: What do you want to recognize?
    • Example: "Rock", "Paper", "Scissors"
  5. Add training data: Use your webcam or upload images
    • Add 30-50 examples per class for best results
  6. Click "Train Model" and wait (usually 10-30 seconds)
  7. Test it! Use the preview to see if it works
  8. Export: Download or get a link to use in projects
‚ö° Pro Tip: The more varied your training data, the better! Different lighting, angles, backgrounds help the AI learn better.

Pro Tips for Better Results

‚úÖ DO These Things:

  • Add lots of examples (30-50 per class minimum)
  • Vary your examples - different angles, lighting, positions
  • Use clear, distinct gestures - make rock VERY different from paper
  • Keep your background consistent during training
  • Test thoroughly - try to trick your AI and see what confuses it
  • Re-train if needed - you can always add more examples and improve it

‚ùå AVOID These Mistakes:

  • Too few examples - 5-10 images won't work well
  • Identical examples - don't just hold still for 50 shots
  • Similar gestures - make each class look different
  • Changing lighting mid-project - confuses the AI
  • Forgetting to test - always verify before saying you're done

Important: AI Isn't Perfect!

‚ö†Ô∏è What You'll Notice:

Your AI will make mistakes! That's normal and actually a great learning opportunity.

Why AI Makes Mistakes:

  • Limited training data - It only knows what you showed it
  • Similar patterns - If two classes look alike, it gets confused
  • New situations - Different lighting, background, or angle can trick it
  • Bias in data - If you only train with your right hand, left hand might not work

What This Teaches Us:

  • Real AI (like facial recognition) can have similar problems
  • AI is only as good as its training data
  • We need to test AI thoroughly before trusting it
  • Understanding limitations is part of being a good AI developer
