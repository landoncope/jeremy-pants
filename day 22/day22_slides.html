New Vocabulary: Technical Terms

Branding
The process of creating a unique identity for a business, product, or person that makes them recognizable and memorable. Branding includes the name, logo, colors, fonts, messaging, and overall "personality" that represents what the business stands for. Think of it like your personal style and reputation, but for a company!

Good branding: When you see the golden arches, you think McDonald's. When you see a swoosh, you think Nike. That's successful branding - instant recognition and association with specific feelings or values.

Why it matters: Your logo is the face of your brand. It's often the first thing people see and remember. Strong branding builds trust, attracts customers, and makes your business stand out from competitors.
Fun Fact: The Nike swoosh logo was designed by a college student in 1971 for only $35! That logo is now recognized worldwide and is valued at billions of dollars. üí∞

New Vocabulary: Technical Terms

Vector Graphics
Images created using mathematical formulas and geometric shapes (points, lines, curves) rather than pixels. Vector graphics can be scaled to ANY size - from business card to billboard - without losing quality or getting blurry. They stay crisp and sharp no matter how much you zoom in!

Vector vs Raster (Pixel):
‚Ä¢ Vector = Made of math (infinitely scalable, small file size, perfect for logos)
‚Ä¢ Raster/Pixel = Made of colored dots (gets blurry when enlarged, good for photos)

Common vector file types: .svg, .ai, .eps
Common raster file types: .jpg, .png, .gif
üí° Pro Tip: Professional logos are ALWAYS created as vector graphics first, then exported to PNG/JPG for web use. This ensures they look perfect everywhere - from tiny app icons to massive banners!

What Makes a Great Web Logo?

The 5 Principles of Good Logo Design:

  • Simple: Easy to recognize and remember (think Apple, Nike, Twitter)
  • Memorable: Stands out and sticks in people's minds
  • Relevant: Fits the business (a law firm ‚â† comic sans and rainbows)
  • Timeless: Won't look outdated in 5-10 years
  • Versatile: Works in color AND black & white, big AND small

+ Web-Specific Requirements:

  • Scalable: Looks sharp from tiny favicon (16x16) to banner size
  • Fast-loading: Simple = smaller file size = faster websites
  • Clean backgrounds: Transparent or solid (no messy edges)

Pro Tips: Writing Web-Ready Logo Prompts

‚ùå Bad Logo Prompt

"grillzilla logo with dinosaur eating bbq realistic 3D shiny"

Too complex! 3D, gradients, and realism don't work well for web logos.

‚úì Good Web Logo Prompt

"Minimalist vector logo for Grillzilla BBQ. Simple geometric dinosaur icon with flames. Flat design, bold red and black, clean lines, transparent background, no gradients, no shadows."

Clean, scalable, web-ready!

Essential Keywords for Web Logos:

  • Style: "minimalist," "flat design," "vector," "simple geometric shapes"
  • Clean Output: "transparent background," "no gradients," "no shadows," "no 3D effects"
  • Web-Ready: "designed for web," "clean lines," "high contrast," "solid colors"
  • Scalability: "simple shapes that scale," "optimized for small sizes"

Common AI Logo Mistakes for Web

‚ùå Don't Do This

  • Gradients, shadows, or 3D effects
  • Photo-realistic or textured styles
  • Too much detail (blurry when small)
  • Messy AI-generated text/spelling
  • Too many colors (2-3 max)
  • White or busy backgrounds

‚úì Do This Instead

  • Flat, solid colors only
  • Vector/geometric style
  • Simple shapes, clean lines
  • Icon only (add text later)
  • 2-3 high-contrast colors
  • Transparent background
Web Logo Test: If your logo doesn't look good at 50x50 pixels (tiny!), it's too complex. Web logos need to be clear at favicon size! üîç
