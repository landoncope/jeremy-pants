New Vocabulary: Technical Terms

Sprite
A 2D image or animation integrated into a larger scene, most commonly used in video games. Sprites represent characters, items, enemies, power-ups, and effects. Think of every character in old-school games like Super Mario, Pokemon, or Stardew Valley - those are all sprites! They're usually smaller images (32x32, 64x64, 128x128 pixels) designed to move around the screen and interact with each other. Modern games still use sprites, especially for 2D games and mobile games.
Fun Fact: The term "sprite" comes from early computer graphics research in the 1970s. Engineers used the word because sprites could move around the screen independently, like little "spirits" or "fairies" floating over the background! üëæ

New Vocabulary: Technical Terms

Generative AI
Artificial Intelligence that creates brand new, original content rather than just analyzing or organizing existing information. Generative AI can produce images, text, music, videos, code, and more by learning patterns from millions of examples during training.

How it works: These AI models are "trained" on huge datasets (like millions of images with descriptions). They learn relationships between concepts - what a "dog" looks like, what "sunset colors" are, what "cartoon style" means. Then when you give it a prompt, it generates something new by combining what it learned in unique ways.

Key difference: Regular AI = recognizes and analyzes (like Siri understanding "set alarm"). Generative AI = creates new things that never existed before.

Examples you use: ChatGPT (text generation), DALL-E/Midjourney (image generation), GitHub Copilot (code generation), Suno (music generation), Runway (video generation)

Writing Good Image Prompts

‚ùå Bad Prompt

"a dog"

Too vague! You'll get random results.

‚úì Good Prompt

"a golden retriever puppy playing in a field of sunflowers, sunny day, digital art style"

Specific details = better results!

Key Elements to Include:

  • Subject: What's the main thing? (dragon, logo, character)
  • Details: Colors, size, mood, action (purple, small, happy, flying)
  • Style: Pixel art, cartoon, realistic, minimalist
  • Setting: Where is it? (forest, space, simple background)
  • Lighting: Bright, dark, neon, sunset (optional but helps!)

Prompt Examples for Your Projects

For Game Sprites:

"pixel art character sprite, brave knight with sword and shield, 64x64, simple colors, transparent background, video game style"
"2D game item icon, glowing blue potion bottle, pixel art, 32x32, simple shading"

For Logos:

"minimalist logo design for tech startup, blue and purple gradient, geometric shapes, modern, clean, simple"
"logo for gaming club, controller icon, bold font, red and black, circular badge design"

For Backgrounds:

"pixel art forest background, sunset colors, parallax scrolling style, side-view platformer game"
"futuristic city skyline at night, neon lights, cyberpunk style, wide format, digital painting"

Pro Tips for Better AI Images

1. Iterate and Refine

Don't expect perfection on the first try! Generate 3-4 versions and pick the best. Then refine your prompt based on what worked.

2. Use Style Keywords

Add style terms like: "pixel art," "cartoon," "realistic," "minimalist," "watercolor," "3D render," "vector art," "anime style"

3. Specify Aspect Ratio

Square (1:1) for icons/sprites, wide (16:9) for backgrounds, tall (9:16) for phone wallpapers

4. Remove Backgrounds

Use remove.bg (free) or "transparent background" in your prompt for sprites/logos

5. Check Licensing

Free tools usually allow personal use. For commercial projects, read the terms or use premium tools.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Don't Do This

  • Using copyrighted characters ("Make me Mario")
  • Prompts with trademarked names ("Nike logo style")
  • Expecting text to work perfectly (AI is bad at spelling)
  • One-word prompts
  • Giving up after one try

‚úì Do This Instead

  • Create original characters ("Italian plumber hero")
  • Describe the style ("swoosh logo, athletic brand")
  • Add text separately in editing software
  • Detailed, specific prompts
  • Try multiple variations!
Fun Fact: AI image generators are terrible at spelling and counting. If you need text or specific numbers, add them yourself using Canva, Photoshop, or even PowerPoint! ‚úçÔ∏è
